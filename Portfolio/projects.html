My Projects

For the PWS project in high school I, together with Maurits, made a physics-based flight simulator. We used c++ and raylib to realistically simulate the flight of a plane. This involved creating a fluid simulation to calculate the aerodynamic forces on the plane, and a general physics simulation to determine the movement of the airplane. We also wrote a paper about the physics that went into making the simulation.

For a project for the coderclass, I decided to make a game for a TI-84 CE-T graphing calculator! Using a c++ toolchain that provides basic graphical features such as sprites and tilemaps. The project was a very fun introduction to game development, and the limitations of the graphing calculator inspire creativity. The game features a player walking in a world with obstacles and chests, having to fight enemies with a sword.
